Largest capacity gains: "Assess coffee quality" (+2.30), "Select ripe cherries" (+2.11), and "Use different processing methods" (+2.00). The weakest improvement was "Keep records of coffee sales" (+0.97), which also has the lowest post-project score (2.51), indicating a remaining gap.

C. Wellbeing Self-rated 1–5 scale, Before vs. Now

Wellbeing Change Detail

IndicatorBeforeNowChange
Wellbeing highlights: "I feel hopeful about my future" saw the largest gain (+1.73, reaching 4.41). Community connection and life satisfaction both rose by +1.27. Financial indicators improved more modestly: savings capacity (+0.70) and income diversity (+0.68) remain the lowest-scoring wellbeing dimensions.

D. Human-Elephant Conflict (HEC) Attitudes Self-rated 1–5 scale, Before vs. Now

Elephant Impact

HEC Attitude Changes

HEC Attitude Detail

AttitudeBeforeNowChange
HEC finding: 65% of respondents have been affected by wild elephants; 32% farm "very close" to the protected forest. "I feel safe from elephant danger" showed the largest attitude shift (+0.92). However, "Elephants are good for our community" barely moved (+0.14, from 1.76 to 1.89), and "Adequate support for elephant protection" remains very low (2.19), pointing to persistent institutional gaps.

E. Most Valuable Benefits Respondents selected up to 3

Top benefits: "Learning new skills" was selected by 78% of respondents, making it the clear standout. "Higher prices" and "Exchange visits" tied at 49%, followed by "Improved quality / Fine Robusta" at 38%. Notably, "Reduced elephant stress" was selected by only 5%, consistent with the modest HEC attitude improvements.
